Marriage in the Catholic church is a sacred bond and a sacrament. Catholic belief and practice has always seen marriage as God’s created institution by which human love and procreation are bound together in an unbreakable covenant for life. Only those couples who genuinely desire a true Catholic Christian marriage and are prepared to accept the duties and obligations that inherent in Christian marriage may be married in the church.

· Pastoral interview is the first step and establishes the readiness of the couple

· Consideration of dates and times will be given at the time of the pastoral interview

· At least a year notice should be considered.

· A Marriage Preparation Session must be completed

R.C.I.A.

 

 

Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults is the process where adults, on listening to the Holy Spirit and God’s call in their heart, consciously and freely seek the living God and enter the way of faith and conversion, to become full members of the Roman Catholic Church and our parish community.

· Persons who are not baptized; those who are baptized in other Christian denominations; and for Catholics who have not received either the Sacrament of the Holy Eucharist or Confirmation

· The process begins with a personal interview to determine a candidates readiness

· Weekly gatherings starting mid September continuing through Easter when Baptism and reception of the Sacrament of Initiation is celebrated at the Easter Vigil.
